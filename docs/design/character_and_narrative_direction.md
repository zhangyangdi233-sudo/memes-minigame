# Character and Narrative Direction

## Purpose

This document keeps every new character and scene inside one original visual and narrative system. The player character designed by the project author is the only character identity reference and the approved `protagonist_operator.png` is the rendering master for the rest of the cast.

External games may inform broad craft principles such as pacing, visual restraint, or the use of silence. Their characters, silhouettes, histories, dialogue, locations, scene order, and specific compositions must not be reproduced.

## Reference Study Notes

The locally installed Ren'Py builds were studied at the level of presentation structure, pacing, and atmosphere. No archive asset or dialogue line is part of this project.

- **Milk outside a bag of milk outside a bag of milk:** the useful lesson is to make presentation inseparable from viewpoint. Ordinary actions and objects keep a scene readable while hesitation, correction, silence, sound, and selective visual disruption reveal subjective pressure. Short acknowledgements can control tempo without pretending every prompt is a major branch.
- **Z.A.T.O. I Love the World and Everything In It:** the useful lesson is the economy of a high-contrast static portrait, restricted color, print-like texture, offset contour, and sparse environmental framing. Those broad graphic principles are filtered through the project owner's protagonist design; no external character identity, costume, pose, or composition is reused.

## Character Style Bible

Use `res://assets/generated/characters/protagonist_operator.png` as the master reference.

- Front-facing, readable full-body silhouette at roughly 5.5 to 6 heads tall, with an oversized head and compact hands and feet.
- Cute anime-cartoon anatomy rather than realism, mature fashion illustration, or painterly rendering.
- Restricted olive green, pale cream, and near-black palette. Pollution may shift values but must not introduce a rainbow cast.
- Clean flat fills with restrained paper grain and minimal soft shading.
- Dark green line art plus a slightly offset cream/white contour that reads like an imperfect print registration.
- NPCs and the merchant have a featureless cream face at source. Their role must remain readable through silhouette, posture, clothing, and props rather than eyes or expressions.
- Clothing is built from one memorable silhouette and two or three functional props. Avoid ornamental detail that disappears at gameplay scale.
- Transparent background with generous clear padding around hair, hands, props, and shoes.

### Cast Roles

- **Operator / protagonist:** long wavy hair, beret, cape-like top, long skirt, phone and notebook. The tired neutral expression is the visual baseline.
- **Frame vendor / merchant:** compact signal clerk with a utility jacket, shoulder strap, frame samples, and a completely blank face. Calm and practical, never mystical exposition in disguise.
- **Late arrival:** small commuter with loose hair, oversized coat, paper ticket, and two watches showing the same impossible minute. Speaks in brief corrections and unfinished time references.
- **Echo tenant:** soft, rounded resident with a cardigan, old keys, listening receiver, and a completely blank face. Uses concrete domestic nouns and repeats one changed word when uncertain.
- **Archive witness:** neat archive clerk with a long coat, document folder, and blank cards orbiting in an almost regular pattern. Speaks in measured clauses and avoids declaring what a record means.

New NPCs must differ in body shape, hair silhouette, clothing function, carried object, contradiction, and conversational rhythm while retaining the rendering grammar above. Apparent age may vary, but realistic aging must never be the source of horror.

## Dialogue Rhythm

- Keep most Chinese lines below 28 visible characters. Longer lines are reserved for loss of control, bureaucratic recitation, or pollution pressure.
- Alternate short observation, pause, correction, and concrete action. Do not explain the whole world in one speech.
- Use ordinary objects to anchor strange events: a ticket, receipt, stair number, wet sleeve, missing label, or battery icon.
- Give each speaker three idiolect rules: preferred sentence length, preferred syntax, and one kind of phrasing they avoid.
- Classify player responses as acknowledgement, inference, or commitment. A true choice must change stored state or receive a later callback.
- Let silence and partial answers remain meaningful. A conversation may be exited before the protagonist speaks and that exit must not consume an action.

## Atmosphere and Presentation

- Reality remains mostly legible. Glitch, signal noise, repeated text, and palette shifts are exceptional punctuation tied to a state change.
- Alternate still observation, compact dialogue, sensory interruption, and recovery. Constant distortion weakens the moments that should feel wrong.
- Attach UI to the dramatic composition: NPC speech remains beside the NPC, the protagonist's thought tiles occupy the lower picture, and the phone belongs to the hand-held viewpoint.
- Give each disturbance one meaning. For example, duplicate outlines indicate inherited language, missing glyphs indicate failed retrieval, and horizontal tearing indicates a pollution threshold crossing.
- Keep face obstruction as a separate animated handwriting layer. The source character image must remain visually intact underneath; only opaque black stroke pixels may cover it, with no rectangular darkening, multiply blend, or destructive texture edit.
- Stage every NPC and merchant as a front-facing camera billboard at the same 1.56 m eye line as the player. The character and handwriting layers share one transform so turning the camera can never separate them.
- Sound should punctuate actions and absences rather than fill every second.

## Originality Checklist

Before accepting a new character or scene, confirm that it has:

1. An original role, history, relationship, vulnerability, and narrative purpose.
2. A silhouette and prop combination not derived one-to-one from any reference character.
3. An original location, action, event order, and reveal structure.
4. Dialogue written from this project's meme pollution and legacy-rule systems.
5. No copied line, name, emblem, costume, UI frame, or recognizable composition from a reference work.
